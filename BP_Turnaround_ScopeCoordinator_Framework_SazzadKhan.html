03Turnaround lifecycle — what TAR means
What is a Turnaround (TAR)?
A Turnaround is the planned, total shutdown of a petrochemical unit for maintenance, inspection, and upgrades that are impossible while the plant is running. Think of it as a Formula 1 pit stop — the plant stops completely so a large coordinated team can perform intense, high-speed work to ensure the plant runs safely for the next operational period.

Every hour the plant is down costs hundreds of thousands of euros in lost production. The Scope Coordinator's data quality directly controls how long the plant stays offline.
Why shutdown is necessary
Regulatory inspection of pressure vessels · Worn part replacement · Control loop recalibration · Catalyst replacement · Debottlenecking upgrades
Coordinator's TAR value
Wrong P&ID data = wrong parts ordered · Hidden scope = shutdown extends · Missing LOTO = fatality risk · Bad loop data = failed restart
TAR planning phases
Long-lead planning (12–18 months) · Scope freeze (3–6 months) · Execution preparation (1–4 weeks) · Execution · Restart
Coordinator's daily role
P&ID walk-down verification · SAP PM data cross-check · LOTO gap identification · Daily Lead Planner briefing · Scope change management

02The 3-component rule — coordinator's loop law
Breaking a loop: why half-scope causes restart failures
A control loop has exactly 3 components: the sensor that feels, the controller that thinks, and the final element that acts. During a shutdown, maintenance on any one component breaks the entire loop. The Scope Coordinator must ensure all 3 have SAP PM work orders — not just the visible valve.
LIC-03 — Level control loop · KO1 column bottom · CRITICAL PATH
LT-03 · Transmitter
Feels the level → LIC-03 · Controller
Thinks / calculates → LV-03 · Control Valve
Acts / opens / closes
Coordinator rule: SAP PM must have 3 separate Maintenance Orders — one per component. If only LV-03 is replaced but LT-03 is not calibrated, the controller receives a wrong signal and the loop fails on restart. One missing MO = one broken loop = one lost production day worth hundreds of thousands of euros.

01LOTO — the legal foundation of turnaround safety
Lockout / Tagout — what it is and why it matters
LOTO is the procedure that ensures all hazardous energy sources connected to equipment are physically isolated and de-energised before any maintenance work begins. In a petrochemical plant, it is a legal requirement — and every piece of equipment in turnaround scope must have an approved LOTO plan before a work order is released to the field.

As Scope Coordinator, my role is not to write the LOTO procedure — that is the safety engineer's task. My role is to ensure the data behind the LOTO plan is complete and accurate. A LOTO plan is only as safe as the P&ID revision it was based on. If the P&ID is wrong or outdated, the isolation is wrong — and that is a preventable fatality.
Lockout
Physical padlock on isolation point. Energy cannot be restored without removing the lock. Always preferred. Used whenever physically possible on any equipment.
Tagout
Warning tag only — "DO NOT OPERATE." Used only when lockout is physically impossible. Provides no physical protection — only a written warning. Coordinator flags all Tagout-only points for review.
Try-out (verify)
After locking, attempt to start the pump or open the valve — prove isolation actually works. Most critical step: isolation is not confirmed without try-out. Missing in many plans.
Group LOTO
Multiple teams on same equipment — each places own lock. Energy cannot be restored until ALL locks removed. Required for SIMOPS scenarios. Coordinator identifies in SAP PM schedule.
02LOTO execution — 6-step coordinator process
01
P&ID isolation trace
Trace every pipe, signal line, and instrument connected to the equipment on the P&ID. Identify all primary isolation valves, Double Block & Bleed positions, drain and vent points, small-bore instrument connections.
02
Energy source identification
List all 6 energy categories: process fluid, electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic, mechanical stored energy, thermal. Cross-reference against SAP PM Equipment Master to confirm all are documented.
03
SAP WCM isolation list
Create isolation list in SAP Work Clearance Management (WCM). Every P&ID valve tag must match a SAP WCM Operational Item. Any mismatch = data inconsistency I must flag immediately to Lead Planner.
04
Blind / spade register
For turnaround work, closed valves alone are insufficient. Physical metal blinds (spades) inserted into pipe flanges create absolute barriers. I maintain the blind register — every blind tracked IN and OUT.
05
Field walk-down verification
Physical plant walk with P&ID and isolation list. Check accessibility — can a technician reach the valve? Check operability — is the valve corroded? Any defect = immediate escalation to Lead Planner.
06
Pre-restart removal log
After work complete: every lock logged out. Isolation-OUT list compared against Isolation-IN list — every entry accounted for. All blinds removed and registered. No restart until list is 100% verified.

04DBB — Double Block and Bleed
When a single valve is not enough
For hazardous petrochemical streams — hydrocarbons, H2S, toxic fluids — a single closed valve is insufficient isolation. Double Block & Bleed requires two closed block valves with a vent (bleed) between them. If the first block valve leaks, the bleed drains pressure between the two blocks and makes the leak visible before it reaches the work zone.

As Scope Coordinator, I identify DBB positions on the P&ID and verify that both block valves AND the bleed valve are listed as separate Operational Items in SAP WCM — not just the primary block valve. Missing the bleed valve from the SAP isolation list is a documented gap I flag immediately.
01The 6 hazardous energy categories
1. Electrical energy
Motor drives, instrumentation power, heat tracing, VFDs, sensor signal cables. Most common in pump/compressor work. PL1AM1 requires isolation at MCC. VT-101 sensor circuit is a separate electrical point not on P&ID Rev.3.
SAP: Motor + separate sensor circuit in WCM
2. Process fluid (chemical)
Pressurised hydrocarbons, toxic streams, acids, caustics. KO1 column: high-temp, high-pressure distillate. Must be depressurised, drained, purged, gas-freed before any opening. Highest risk category in petrochemical plants.
SAP: Depressurisation + drain + purge prereqs
3. Thermal energy
Hot process fluids, steam, heat-traced lines. KO1 column bottom carries hot bottoms product. Specific cool-down time must appear on the SAP work order as a prerequisite condition before opening.
SAP: Temperature confirmation prereq on WO
4. Pneumatic energy
Compressed air and instrument gas. Control valve actuators on YCZ and FV-02 are pneumatically driven — must be depressurised before removal. Instrument air header isolation required and listed in WCM.
SAP: Instrument air isolation in WCM
5. Mechanical stored energy
Compressed springs in valve assemblies (relief valve springs, check valves), counterweights, suspended loads. Must be blocked or restrained before removal. Spring-loaded PSVs require special handling procedure.
SAP: Blocking method specified in WO scope
6. Hydraulic energy
Pressurised hydraulic systems in large valve actuators and control systems. Less common but critical for large actuated valve work. Requires formal depressurisation procedure and fluid drain before work begins.
SAP: Hydraulic depressurisation prereq on WO
02Gas-freeing — mandatory before KO1 vessel entry
Gas-freeing protocol — KO1 distillation column entry
Gas-freeing is the removal of all hazardous vapours from a vessel and replacement with breathable air — below the Lower Explosive Limit (LEL) and Occupational Exposure Limit (OEL). For KO1, this is mandatory because distillation columns contain flammable hydrocarbon vapours and potentially H2S or benzene at toxic concentrations.

Three fatal risks if gas-freeing is skipped:
1. Flammability — hydrocarbon vapours at 1–5% concentration form explosive mixture with air
2. Toxicity — H2S at 50 ppm causes rapid incapacitation; benzene is carcinogenic at any exposure level
3. Oxygen deficiency — after nitrogen purge, oxygen content may fall below 19.5% — causing immediate unconsciousness

03Inadvertent energy restoration — data prevents it
The most preventable turnaround fatality
Inadvertent energy restoration — a valve opened by mistake on a supposedly isolated line — is caused not by equipment failure but by data failure: incorrect isolation lists, unregistered energy points, work orders released without complete LOTO documentation.

Three data defences as Scope Coordinator:

1. XVERWEIS cross-check: Every P&ID valve tag cross-referenced against SAP PM Equipment Master. Any valve with no SAP record has no LOTO tag — it can be operated by anyone unaware that work is in progress nearby. My XVERWEIS validation catches every tag mismatch before execution starts.

2. Field inconsistency detection: Walk-downs catch equipment added since the last P&ID revision — like VT-101 on PL1AM1 and the MOV at KO1. These undocumented energy sources are the exact ones that cause incidents.

3. Work order scope clarity: SAP PM WOs must specify not only what IS done but what must NOT be done — which adjacent valves, switches, and connections are off-limits because neighbouring teams are working.
01Permit to Work — types and coordinator role
Hot Work Permit
Heißarbeitserlaubnis
Required for any activity creating ignition sources: welding, grinding, cutting. Must include gas monitoring, fire watch, fire suppression on standby, and minimum exclusion zone from open hydrocarbons.
Coordinator: verify no open equipment or nozzle within 10m — check P&ID for all open vents in area
Cold Work Permit
Kaltarbeitserlaubnis
For mechanical and instrument work with no ignition sources. Still requires LOTO compliance and gas monitoring. Default permit for most turnaround mechanical work: seal replacement, bearing service, calibration.
Coordinator: default permit for mechanical scope — verify LOTO complete before PTW issued
Confined Space Entry
Befahrerlaubnis
Any work inside vessel, tank, column, or enclosed space. Requires gas-free certificate, standby person outside, rescue equipment pre-positioned, communication system established.
Coordinator: gas-free cert must appear in SAP WO prerequisites — NO entry without confirmed cert
Electrical Isolation Permit
Elektrische Isolationserlaubnis
Required for all electrical work: motor service, instrument loop break, sensor replacement. Separate from mechanical LOTO. Electrical team issues their own lock on the MCC.
Coordinator: verify electrical work center assigned in SAP WO — separate lock required from Electrical
Excavation / Height Permit
Erdarbeiten / Höhenarbeiten
Required for below-grade piping work or elevated work requiring scaffolding. Scaffolding requirements identified during field walk-down and added to SAP scope as prerequisite activity.
Coordinator: identify scaffolding needs during P&ID walk-down — add to SAP before WO release
02SIMOPS — simultaneous operations risk management
SIMOPS identification — the coordinator's data-driven check
SIMOPS occurs when two or more different work activities happen simultaneously in the same area or on interconnected equipment. In a turnaround with hundreds of concurrent work orders, unmanaged SIMOPS creates the most severe safety conflicts — especially when one activity involves ignition sources and another involves opening equipment with residual hydrocarbons.

Classic SIMOPS example: Hot Work (welding) on a flange 10 metres from where Electrical is opening a motor terminal box with residual hydrocarbon vapour. If the permit issuer does not know about both activities simultaneously, neither permit includes mitigations for the combined risk.

Coordinator's SAP PM SIMOPS check: Review all work orders with overlapping time windows and adjacent Functional Locations. Any Hot Work order within 10m of an open-equipment order = SIMOPS flag to Lead Planner.

01SAP PM — the digital ledger of every bolt and pipe
Functional Location
The "Address" of the position
Permanent address in the plant — exists whether or not a physical machine is installed. Does not change even if the pump is replaced. Every P&ID tag must map to a FuncLoc. Any tag without a FuncLoc = safety data gap.
Equipment Master
The "ID Card" of the machine
Serial number, manufacturer, installation date, full technical specs of the physical machine. Used to verify field reality matches SAP. A mismatch = data inconsistency. No Equipment Master entry = no LOTO tag possible.
Maintenance Order (MO)
The "Work Authorisation"
Formal authorisation for work — allocates work center, assigns man-hours, attaches materials, carries cost budget. Work cannot officially start without an approved MO. Must include permit type and all safety prerequisites.
Work Center
The "Team" doing the work
The discipline assigned — Mechanical, Electrical, Instrument, Civil. A control valve like YCZ requires two work centers. Coordinator verifies both are assigned and no work center overlap creates a SIMOPS conflict.
Notification
The "Finding Report"
Report of a finding — describes a condition, defect, or requirement. Does not authorise work or allocate budget. Used when a field inconsistency requires Lead Planner review before scope decision. Converts to Work Order on approval.
TECO Status
The "Technical Complete" flag
Technically Complete — physical work confirmed done. I cross-check every TECO order against the P&ID: did the team address all 3 loop components, or just the visible valve? Premature TECO on safety-critical item = immediate escalation.
